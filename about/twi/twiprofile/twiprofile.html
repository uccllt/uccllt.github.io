GENERAL INTRODUCTION

Of all the dialects of the Akan language, Asante Twi is the most prevelant. Asante Twi spoken by an estimated 2.8 million people, over 40 percent of Ghana’s population, as their first language. Due to its popularity and being originated from the Ashanti Province, mainly south central Ghana, Asante Twi is easily understood by speakers of other Akan dialects. Asante Twi is also spoken parts of the Eastern, Western, Center and Volta and Brong Ahafo Regions of Ghana. There are also small numbers of Akan people in south eastern Cte d I’voire. LINGUISTIC AFFILIATION

Asante Twi belongs the Kwa subfamily of the Niger-Congo language family. It is the most widely spoken of the Akan dialects. Throughout Ghana, Akan is spoken by over 40 percent of the population as a first language by people living in the following regions: Ashanti, and parts of the Eastern, Western, Central, Volta, and Brong Ahafo......↩

Twi is a linguistic term designating a language which belongs to the Akan branch of the Kwa subfamily of the Niger-Congo language family (Greenberg 1966: 8; Manoukian 1950: 10). The Twi-speaking peoples, who are concentrated in southern Ghana (formerly the British colony of the Gold Coast), include the Akwamu, the Akwampim (Akuapem), the Akyem (Akim), the Asen-Twifo, the Ashanti (Asante), the Fanti, the Kwahu, and the Wasa......↩

LANGUAGE VARIATION

Within the Akan languages, Twi is at times used a blanket term for the various dialects that exist. Ghananians do not speak Akan, they speak Akan dialects. There are three dialects that are prevalent: Asante Twi, Akuapem Twi and Fante. All are mutually intelligible......↩
