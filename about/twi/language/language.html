The following language lessons were developed using Hot Potatoes by Selassie Ahorlu based on the Twi language text book by Florence Abena Dolphyne "A Comprehensive Course in Twi (Asante) for the Non-Twi Learner"(Accra: Ghana Universities Press, 1996). This is the textbook frequently used by University of California Education Abroad Students who study Twi while in Ghana on their study program. They could be used in conjunction with an in-class course with an instructor, or by students who are studying Twi on their own. While the exercises have been designed to be used along with Dolphyne's text, they can be used independently. Students can also use the exercises to gauge their own progress.

NB: In order to view the special characters used in the Twi orthography, namely ɛ and ɔ, your system should have installed in its font resource folder Unicode Lucida Grande. The exercises have been tested on Safari and Explorer.

There are, for the most part, two types of exercises used in these drills. One is multiple choice questions; the other is drop and drag exercises. In the former the correct answer is selected by checking the correct answer; in the latter a matching item is selected with the mouse and dragged across the screen and dropped next to the correct item in the exercise. Both types have a feedback feature that allows the student to check results immediately.
