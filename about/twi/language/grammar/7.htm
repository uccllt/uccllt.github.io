Grammar Exercise. The Various Uses of DE "to take" and "to be named/carry a name/be called". To review the differences in usage of DE see pages 29, 47-48 and page 98 in Dolphyne's textbook. Also see examples of each in Dialogue 4, p. 12, Dialogue 9, p. 28 and Dialogue 14, p. 46. DE can be used in serial verb constructions (see Dialogue 9, pp. 28-29) or in the idiom used to express affective states, e.g. "being hungry" etc. (see Dialogue 14 and its grammar note, pp. 46-47). It is also used in naming (see Dialogue 4, p. 12). Translate the following English sentences using DE properly.

Quiz

 

  1. I am thirsty.

    1.   Me de sukɔm.
    2.   Me wɔ sukɔm.
    3.   Sukɔm de me.
    4.   Me yɛ sukɔm.

  2. My name is Kofi.

    1.   Kofi de me.
    2.   Me wɔ din Kofi.
    3.   Me yɛ Kofi.
    4.   Me de Kofi.

  3. I am feeling hot/warm.

    1.   Me wɔ ahuhuro.
    2.   Ahuhuro me de.
    3.   Ahuhuro de me.
    4.   Me yɛ ahuhuro

  4. We are feeling hot.

    1.   Yɛ de ahuhuro
    2.   Ahuhuro de yɛn
    3.   Ahuhuro wɔ yɛn
    4.   Yɛ yɛ ahuhuro

  5. I'm bringing the food here

    1.   Mede aduane no reba ha
    2.   Aduane no de me reba ha.
    3.   Me yɛ aduane ba ha.
    4.   Me wɔ aduan wɔ ha .

  6. I'm cold.

    1.   Me wɔ awɔ.
    2.   Awɔ de me.
    3.   Me de awɔ.
    4.   Me yɛ awɔ.

  7. He is hungry.

    1.   Ɔyɛ ɛkɔm.
    2.   Ɔde ɛkɔm.
    3.   Ɛkɔm de no.
    4.   Ɛde no ɛkɔm.

  8. She wants to "pee".

    1.   Dwonsɔ de no
    2.   Dwonsɔ yɛ no.
    3.   Ɔde dwonsɔ.
    4.   Dwonsɔ yɛ de no.

  9. His name is Kwame.

    1.   Ɔyɛ Kwame.
    2.   Kwame de no din.
    3.   Kwame de no.
    4.   Ɔde Kwame.

  10. I brought it.

    1.   Me bae.
    2.   Me de bae.
    3.   Ɛde me bae.
    4.   Me bae de me.

  11. I'm taking the water there.

    1.   Me de nsuo no rekɔ hɔ.
    2.   Nsuo no de me rekɔ hɔ.
    3.   Me de nsuo de rekɔ hɔ.
    4.   Me de rekɔ nsuo hɔ.

  12. He will give it to her.

    1.   Bɛ ma no de.
    2.   Ɔbɛ de ma no.
    3.   Ɔde bɛ ma no.
    4.   Ma no na ɔde.

  13. She will bring it.

    1.   Ɔde bɛ ba.
    2.   Ɔbɛ brɛ ɛno.
    3.   Bɛ ba ɔde.
    4.   Bɛ brɛ no.

  14. I'm taking you home.

    1.   Wo rekɔ me de wo.
    2.   Me wo rekɔ fie.
    3.   Me de wo rekɔ fie.
    4.   Ɛfie na wo rekɔ.

  15. He gave it to me.

    1.   Ɔbɛ de ama me.
    2.   Ɔde bɛ ma me.
    3.   Ɔde maa me.
    4.   Maa me na ɔdeɛ.
