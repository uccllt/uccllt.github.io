California Language Roadmap Summit

The University of California Consortium for Language Learning & Teaching (Robert Blake, UC Davis, director) and the Language Acquisition Research Center (Mary Ann Lyman-Hager, San Diego State University, director) received funding from the National Security Education Program (NSEP) to develop a roadmap for the study of world languages in the state of California. Other states that have held NSEP-sponsored Language Roadmap summits are Ohio, Oregon, Texas, and Utah.

The first step toward developing the California roadmap was a summit meeting "Building World Citizens for California`s Success" held in San Diego on May 7th, 2010. The planning committee included the California Foreign Language Project (Duarte Silva, Stanford University) and the California State University Strategic Language Initiative (Kim Oanh Nguyen-Lam, CSU Long Beach). The committee invited representatives from community organizations, education, business, and government to participate in the summit meeting and begin to formulate an action agenda for policy makers and educational leaders with the goal of positioning California for leadership in education and economic and political success in the 21st century.

Former astronaut Jim Neuman, former U.S. ambassador David Lyon, Peter Wang, president and CEO of the Wang Foundation, and former provost of the Defense Language Institute Susan Steele were among the keynote speakers who reflected on the pivotal role of language competence in their professional life and pressed for expansion and enhancement of language and culture education in California schools.

Roundtable discussions then formulated and proposed next steps in the effort to design the California Language Roadmap. The summit meeting established a roadmap task force to carry out a series of short and long-term actions, including: identifying a champion in the legislature who would sponsor a bill to provide loan forgiveness or other incentives to encourage language learning; conducting a Corporate Leadership Forum to establish a public/private partnership committed to educating the public on the need for linguistic and cultural competence in the workforce of the future; establishing a commonly recognized Global Citizenship Seal of Excellence for K-12 schools; developing a post-secondary certification plan to document job applicants` language and cultural competence.

Due to the size and complexity of the State of California, the May summit was just the first step in the design of the California Language Roadmap. Next steps involve identifying advocates in business, education, and government who will promote implementation of roadmap proposals. A second summit meeting will convene in September 2010. The slogan for the California Language Roadmap, "World Language will be a mandatory component throughout grades K-12 and every California college graduate will attain functional competence in a language in addition to English by 2025," will frame the ongoing efforts of the task force. The UC Language Consortium website (http://uccllt.ucdavis.edu/) will track the progress of the California Language Roadmap.
